Extended-Release vs Sublingual Buprenorphine in Pregnancy through 12-months Postpartum

Winhusen TJ et al. — JAMA Internal Medicine (2026)

Weekly injectable XR-buprenorphine led to significantly higher illicit opioid abstinence rates than sublingual buprenorphine during pregnancy, with fewer serious adverse events. First RCT of XR-bup in pregnancy.

First evidence supporting LAI buprenorphine as a safe, superior option for OUD in pregnancy — immediate clinical application.

OUD pregnancy buprenorphine Source
2 Candidate

Low-Dose Semaglutide for Alcohol Use Disorder

JAMA Psychiatry (2025)

Semaglutide reduced alcohol self-administration and weekly craving vs placebo in 48 adults with AUD over 9 weeks.

GLP-1 agonists may represent the most important pharmacologic advance in addiction since buprenorphine. Multiple ongoing trials with results expected 2025–2027.

pharmacotherapy AUD novel mechanisms
3 Candidate

Hospital Addiction Consultation Service and Opioid Use Disorder Treatment (START RCT)

Ober AJ, Murray-Krezan C, Page K, Friedmann PD et al. — JAMA Internal Medicine (2025)

START intervention: 57.3% initiated MOUD in hospital vs 26.7% usual care; 72% linked to OUD care post-discharge vs 48.1%.

Strong RCT evidence supporting hospital-based addiction consult services — directly applicable to inpatient settings.

MOUD hospital consult service Source
4 Candidate

Smoking Cessation Improves SUD Recovery

Parks et al. (2025)

Quitting smoking increased odds of SUD recovery up to 42-fold in over 2,600 adults. Challenges the belief that tobacco cessation should be deferred during addiction treatment.

Tobacco cessation is not a threat to recovery — it's part of recovery. Practice-changing for programs that defer nicotine treatment.

tobacco SUD recovery integrated treatment
5 Candidate

AI-Based OUD Screening in Hospitalized Adults

Afshar M et al. — Nature Medicine (2025)

AI screening tool clinically implemented to identify OUD risk in hospitalized patients — bridging technology and addiction care.

Represents the intersection of technology and addiction medicine; could improve early identification and treatment initiation.

AI/ML OUD screening hospital Source
6 Candidate

Healthcare Provider Stigma Toward Patients with Substance Use Disorders

Parish C et al. — Addiction (2025)

Stigma highest for stimulant use disorders, then OUD, then AUD among PCPs and ED physicians. Lower stigma associated with providing MOUD and having SUD consultation available.

Quantifies the barrier we all face. Makes the case for addiction consultation services and integrated training.

stigma provider attitudes consult service Source
7 Candidate

Rethinking Medication Treatment Goals for OUD

McLellan & Volkow

Proposes a three-stage framework: protection → remission → recovery. Argues against medication tapering as default goal; supports lifelong MOUD when appropriate.

Paradigm shift from "taper to abstinence" model. Directly relevant to discharge planning in ASAM 3.7/3.5 settings.

MOUD treatment philosophy recovery model
8 Candidate

TMS for Stimulant Use Disorders (STIMULUS Trial)

Multi-site RCT — Protocol published May 2025

Ongoing double-blind sham-controlled trial (n=160) of 10 Hz TMS to left DLPFC for cocaine/methamphetamine use disorder. Results pending.

If positive, could be first FDA-cleared neuromodulation treatment for stimulant addiction — a condition with no approved pharmacotherapy.

neuromodulation stimulant use disorder clinical trials
9 Candidate

AMA 2025 Report on Substance Use and Treatment

American Medical Association (2025)

Overdose deaths declined from 110,000 (2023) to ~75,000 (2024). Buprenorphine prescriptions up 83% over decade but plateauing. Highlights emerging threats: xylazine, kratom, tianeptine.

Comprehensive policy and epidemiological overview — good for big-picture context discussions.

epidemiology policy emerging threats Source
10 Candidate

Substance Use Disorder and Mortality in US Immigration Detention (2018–2025)

Tirado B et al. — Journal of Addiction Medicine (2025)

Among 27 deaths involving probable SUD in ICE custody, none had documented SUD diagnosis, referral, or evidence-based treatment. 9 died from untreated withdrawal complications.

Harrowing documentation of system failure. Raises ethics of withholding addiction treatment in institutional settings.

About

Journal Club Format

Each article receives its own interactive single-page dashboard designed for group discussion and clinical teaching. Articles are selected for their relevance to addiction medicine practice, with a focus on evidence that can change how we think and what we do.

Teaching Context

Designed for addiction medicine teaching at ASAM 3.7 (medically managed intensive inpatient) and 3.5 (clinically managed high-intensity residential) levels of care. Content supports multidisciplinary staff education and clinical decision-making.

Dashboard Sections

Each article dashboard includes structured sections:

  • Clinical question & study overview
  • Key findings with data visualization
  • Strengths & limitations analysis
  • Clinical implications for practice
  • Discussion questions for group review
